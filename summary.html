MUSIC HISTORY
HISTORY OF
MUSIC
Ancient music spans diverse cultures such as Mesopotamia, Egypt, Greece, and China. Mesopotamian music dates back to 1400 BCE with early notation. Egyptian music was linked to ceremonies, using harps and flutes. Greek music, influential for its theory, explored scales and intervals.
MUSIC HISTORY
MEDIEVAL AND
RENAISSANCE MUSIC
Medieval music (500-1400 CE) featured Gregorian chant and the rise of polyphony, with motets and madrigals. The Renaissance (1400-1600 CE) saw increased harmony and secular music. Composers like Josquin des Prez and Palestrina were pivotal, and the printing press spread musical scores widely.

MUSIC HISTORY
20TH CENTURY AND
CONTEMPORARY MUSIC
The 20th century brought diverse styles, including Impressionism, atonal music, jazz, rock 'n' roll, and electronic music. Key figures included Debussy, Schoenberg, and Elvis Presley. Contemporary music remains eclectic, integrating digital technology and global influences.
MUSIC HISTORY
PHILIPPINE
MUSICAL INDUSTRY
Philippine music blends indigenous, Spanish, American, and other influences. Indigenous music uses instruments like the kulintang. Spanish colonization introduced forms like harana. The American period brought jazz and rock. Modern Original Pilipino Music (OPM) showcases a mix of traditional and contemporary styles.
